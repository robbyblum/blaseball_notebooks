Analysis Summary

Brief summary of my opinions about what we should do this election:

  • We should probably Amplify who we think we want to Charge, in their primary stat category: hitting for someone like Monique, pitching for someone like Callum.
  • Reject the peanut. It brings only pain.
  • Acquire the Fist of the Ape God. It brings power.
  • We could try to reroll some hitters if we want, which at least will rid us Rylan's current horrendous attribute line. We could try to reroll some pitchers, but it won't hit Eliot, so it doesn't feel worth it to me.
  • The blood blessings don't seem worth it, too high a likelihood of landing on someone bad and being wasted.
  • The snow items might be nice; at least, the pitching and hitting ones anyway.

Amplification Analysis

Since we're bad this season, and this is not going to get us into the playoffs, Well, we're at .500, somehow, and still in playoff contention. If we want a postseason birth, we should go for something with high impact, which is either pitching or hitting. Even if somehow we don't care about that, it probably still makes the most sense to go for one that would most benefit a player we want to static. This is most likely going to be either Monique Flowchart, our best hitter (2nd in the league in OPS+), or Callum Toliver, our best pitcher (7th in the league in ERA+).

If we want to static Monique, then it would be best to choose the hitting boost. The baserunning boost looks larger on paper but I strongly doubt it would be better to go for in this case; hitting is just so much more impactful. If we want to static Callum, then we'd want the pitching boost.

If we win a Distortion that maxes out another player, say, then our plans about Static could change, but since they're a raffle we shouldn't plan on that.

Cold Call

Call the Alternate for the 2 Worst Pitchers in your Team's Rotation.

An Alternate is a reroll of the player's stats. I believe that last time around, these rerolls maintained combined star total in the way that expansion-era "purple alternates" did, but since they hit actually bad players they didn't have the odd drawbacks of those. This should also reroll players if they are still the worst player after their first reroll. See below for the list of players this would reroll. For reference, the statistical average reroll would have ~1.5 pitching stars (or ~0.3 Pitching Rating).

Analysis:

To my immense disappointment, Eliot Schofield has managed to eat just enough snow boosts to become our third lowest-starred pitcher, despite definitely still being our worst pitcher. This is awful! Olive and Nolan are bad, but they've somehow scraped themselves up to about 92 ERA+. The league median ERA+ is about 107, so the expected value of a reroll is probably still positive, but rerolling our 3rd and 4th worst pitchers while letting our undeniably worst pitcher keep doing his thing feels really silly to me. If we get a pitching amplification, the order could easily still change, here, and the boost might be "wasted" to some extent.
